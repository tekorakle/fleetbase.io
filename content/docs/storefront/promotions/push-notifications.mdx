# Push Notifications

Push notifications are a powerful tool for re-engaging customers, announcing new products, and driving immediate sales through [Promotions](/docs/storefront/promotions).

Storefront includes a built-in push notification engine that allows you to send targeted messages directly to your customers' mobile devices.

## Prerequisites

To send push notifications, you must first configure a notification provider.

Storefront officially supports **Firebase Cloud Messaging (FCM)** and **Apple Push Notification service (APNs)**.

1. Navigate to **Storefront** > **Settings** > **Notifications** in the Fleetbase Console.
2. Select your provider (e.g., FCM).
3. Enter your server credentials (e.g., your Firebase Server Key).
4. Click **Save**.

Your frontend application (such as the [Storefront App](/docs/apps/storefront-app)) must also be configured to request notification permissions from the user and register their device token with the [Storefront API](/docs/api-reference/storefront).

## Sending a Broadcast Notification

A broadcast notification is sent to all registered customers who have opted in to receive push notifications.

To send a broadcast:

1. Navigate to **Storefront** > **Promotions** > **Push Notifications** in the Fleetbase Console.
2. Click the **New Notification** button.
3. Provide the details:
   - **Title**: The bold headline of the notification (e.g., "Flash Sale! 🍕").
   - **Body**: The main text of the message (e.g., "Get 20% off all pizzas for the next 2 hours. Use code PIZZA20 at checkout.").
4. (Optional) Attach an **Image**: A rich media image to display alongside the notification.
5. (Optional) Set a **Deep Link**: A specific URL or route within your app that the notification should open when tapped (e.g., `myapp://category/pizza`).
6. Click **Send**.

Storefront will queue the notification and dispatch it to all eligible devices via your configured provider.

## Targeted Notifications

Instead of broadcasting to everyone, you can send targeted notifications to specific segments of your customer base.

When creating a new notification, you can select specific [Customers](/docs/storefront/customers) from the list.

This is useful for:
- Re-engaging customers who haven't placed an order in the last 30 days.
- Sending a special offer to your top 10% most frequent buyers.
- Apologizing for a service disruption to a specific group of users.

## Automated Transactional Notifications

In addition to marketing broadcasts, Storefront automatically sends transactional push notifications to keep customers informed about their active orders.

These include:
- "Your order has been accepted and is being prepared."
- "Your driver is on the way!"
- "Your order has been delivered. Enjoy!"

These automated notifications are triggered by status changes in the underlying FleetOps Order (see [Orders & Checkout](/docs/storefront/orders)). You do not need to manually configure these, provided your notification provider is set up correctly.
