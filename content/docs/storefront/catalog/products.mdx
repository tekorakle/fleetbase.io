# Products

A **Product** is the core entity of your catalog. It represents a single item or service that a customer can purchase from a Store.

Every product in Storefront is tied to a specific Store. If you operate a multi-vendor Network, each Store manages its own unique list of products.

## Creating a Product

To add a new product to your catalog:

1. Navigate to **Storefront** > **Products** in the Fleetbase Console.
2. Ensure you have selected the correct Store from the context switcher at the top of the screen.
3. Click the **New Product** button.
4. Provide the basic details:
   - **Name**: The public-facing name of the product (e.g., "Classic Cheeseburger").
   - **Description**: A detailed description of the item, including ingredients, materials, or features.
   - **Price**: The base price of the product in the Store's currency.
   - **SKU**: (Optional) A Stock Keeping Unit identifier for your internal tracking.
5. Upload an **Image**: A high-quality photo of the product. This is crucial for driving conversions in your frontend application.
6. Assign the product to one or more **Categories** (e.g., "Burgers", "Lunch Specials").
7. Click **Save**.

## Product Status

You can control the visibility of a product using its **Status** toggle.

- **Active**: The product is visible in your frontend application and customers can add it to their cart.
- **Inactive**: The product is hidden from the frontend application. This is useful if an item is temporarily out of stock or out of season.

## Advanced Configuration

While a basic product only needs a name, description, and price, Storefront supports more complex configurations:

### [Variants & Add-ons](/docs/storefront/catalog/variants-and-addons)
If your product comes in different sizes, colors, or allows for customization (e.g., "Extra Cheese"), you can configure these options.

### [Pricing Rules](/docs/storefront/catalog/pricing)
If you need to offer bulk discounts, wholesale pricing, or dynamic pricing based on the customer's location, you can configure advanced pricing rules.

## The Product Payload

When a customer purchases a product, Storefront automatically generates an Order in FleetOps.

The details of the purchased product (Name, SKU, Quantity, Price) are passed to FleetOps and stored in the Order's **Payload**. This ensures that your dispatchers and drivers have a complete record of what needs to be delivered, without needing to cross-reference the Storefront catalog.
