# Variants & Addons

Not all products are simple, single-price items. Many products require the customer to make choices before adding them to their cart.

Storefront handles these choices using two distinct concepts: **Variants** and **Add-ons**.

## Variants (Mutually Exclusive Choices)

A **Variant** represents a specific version of a product. When a product has variants, the customer *must* choose exactly one variant to purchase.

Common examples of variants include:
- **Size**: Small, Medium, Large
- **Color**: Red, Blue, Green
- **Material**: Cotton, Polyester

### Creating Variants

To add variants to a product:

1. Navigate to **Storefront** > **Products** in the Fleetbase Console.
2. Open the product you want to modify.
3. Go to the **Variants** tab.
4. Click **Add Variant**.
5. Provide the details:
   - **Name**: The name of the variant (e.g., "Large").
   - **Price**: The specific price for this variant. *Note: When a product has variants, the base product price is often ignored in favor of the variant price.*
   - **SKU**: (Optional) A unique identifier for this specific variant.
6. Click **Save**.

When a customer views this product in your frontend application, they will be presented with a dropdown or radio buttons to select their desired variant.

## Add-ons (Optional Extras)

An **Add-on** represents an optional extra or upgrade that a customer can add to a product. Unlike variants, a customer can choose multiple add-ons, or none at all.

Common examples of add-ons include:
- **Food Modifiers**: "Extra Cheese (+$1.00)", "Add Bacon (+$1.50)", "No Onions"
- **Upgrades**: "Gift Wrapping (+$5.00)", "Extended Warranty (+$50.00)"

### Creating Add-ons

To add add-ons to a product:

1. Navigate to **Storefront** > **Products** in the Fleetbase Console.
2. Open the product you want to modify.
3. Go to the **Add-ons** tab.
4. Click **Add Add-on**.
5. Provide the details:
   - **Name**: The name of the add-on (e.g., "Extra Cheese").
   - **Price**: The additional cost of this add-on. If the add-on is free (e.g., "No Onions"), set the price to `0`.
6. Click **Save**.

When a customer views this product in your frontend application, they will be presented with a list of checkboxes to select their desired add-ons.

## The Cart Payload

When a customer adds a product to their cart with selected variants and add-ons, the [Storefront API](/docs/api-reference/storefront) automatically calculates the total price.

For example, if a customer selects a "Large" variant ($10.00) and adds "Extra Cheese" ($1.00) and "Bacon" ($1.50), the total price for that line item in the cart will be $12.50.

When the customer completes checkout, these selections are passed to FleetOps and stored in the Order's **Payload**, ensuring the merchant knows exactly how to prepare the item.
