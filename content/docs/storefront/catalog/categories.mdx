# Categories

A **Category** is a logical grouping of [Products](/docs/storefront/catalog/products).

Categories are essential for organizing your catalog and making it easy for customers to navigate your Storefront application.

## Creating a Category

To add a new category to your catalog:

1. Navigate to **Storefront** > **Categories** in the Fleetbase Console.
2. Ensure you have selected the correct Store from the context switcher at the top of the screen.
3. Click the **New Category** button.
4. Provide the basic details:
   - **Name**: The public-facing name of the category (e.g., "Appetizers", "T-Shirts", "Electronics").
   - **Description**: A brief description of the category.
5. Upload an **Image**: A high-quality photo representing the category. This is often used as a thumbnail in your frontend application's navigation menu.
6. Click **Save**.

## Assigning Products to Categories

Once you have created a category, you can assign products to it.

1. Navigate to **Storefront** > **Products** in the Fleetbase Console.
2. Open the product you want to categorize.
3. In the **Categories** field, select the appropriate category from the dropdown list.
4. Click **Save**.

A single product can belong to multiple categories. For example, a "Cheeseburger" might belong to both the "Burgers" category and the "Lunch Specials" category.

## Subcategories (Nested Categories)

Storefront supports hierarchical categories, allowing you to create complex catalog structures.

For example, you might have a top-level category called "Clothing", with subcategories for "Men's", "Women's", and "Children's".

To create a subcategory:

1. Create a new category as described above.
2. In the **Parent Category** field, select the top-level category you want to nest this new category under.
3. Click **Save**.

When you fetch categories via the [Storefront API](/docs/api-reference/storefront), the response will include the hierarchical structure, allowing your frontend application to render nested navigation menus or breadcrumbs.

## Category Ordering

The order in which categories are displayed in your frontend application is determined by the **Order** field.

You can manually set the order of your categories in the Fleetbase Console to ensure that your most popular or highest-margin categories appear first.
