# Catalog Management

The **Catalog** is the heart of your Storefront. It contains all the items you sell, how they are organized, and how much they cost.

Because Storefront is designed to support both simple retail and complex food delivery, the catalog system is highly flexible.

## Core Catalog Entities

A Store's catalog is built using three main entities:

### 1. [Products](/docs/storefront/catalog/products)
The individual items available for purchase (e.g., "Cheeseburger", "Nike T-Shirt", "1 Hour Plumber Service"). Every product belongs to a specific Store.

### 2. [Categories](/docs/storefront/catalog/categories)
The organizational structure used to group products together (e.g., "Appetizers", "Main Courses", "Drinks"). Categories make it easy for customers to navigate your store in the frontend application.

### 3. [Variants & Add-ons](/docs/storefront/catalog/variants-and-addons)
Modifiers that allow customers to customize a product before adding it to their cart.
- **Variants** are mutually exclusive choices (e.g., Size: Small, Medium, Large).
- **Add-ons** are optional extras (e.g., "Add Bacon +$1.50", "Extra Ketchup").

## Managing the Catalog

You can manage your catalog directly from the Fleetbase Console.

1. Navigate to **Storefront** > **Products** or **Categories**.
2. Ensure you have selected the correct **Store** from the context switcher at the top of the screen (if you manage multiple stores).
3. Use the interface to create, edit, and organize your items.

## Headless Catalog API

If you are building a custom frontend, you will use the [Storefront API](/docs/api-reference/storefront) to fetch the catalog data and render it for the customer.

A typical flow looks like this:

1. **Fetch Categories**: Retrieve the list of categories for a specific Store to build the navigation menu.
2. **Fetch Products**: When a customer clicks a category, retrieve the products belonging to that category.
3. **Fetch Product Details**: When a customer clicks a specific product, retrieve its full details, including its available Variants and Add-ons, so the customer can customize their order before adding it to the cart.

## Inventory Management

Currently, Storefront does not have a built-in, real-time inventory tracking system (e.g., "Only 5 shirts left in stock").

If a product is out of stock, the Store manager must manually toggle the product's **Status** to `Inactive` in the Fleetbase Console. This will immediately hide the product from the frontend application, preventing customers from ordering it.

*Note: If you require advanced warehouse and inventory management, consider using the [Pallet](/docs/pallet) extension in conjunction with Storefront.*
