# Orders & Checkout

The primary purpose of Storefront is to facilitate transactions. The **Orders & Checkout** system handles the entire lifecycle of a purchase, from the moment a customer adds an item to their cart to the moment the order is dispatched for delivery.

## The Transaction Lifecycle

A typical transaction in Storefront moves through three distinct phases:

### 1. [The Cart](/docs/storefront/orders/cart)
The Cart is a temporary container for the items a customer intends to purchase.
- Customers can add [Products](/docs/storefront/catalog/products), specify quantities, and select [Variants & Add-ons](/docs/storefront/catalog/variants-and-addons).
- The Cart automatically calculates the subtotal based on the configured [Pricing](/docs/storefront/catalog/pricing).
- Carts are persisted in the database, meaning a customer can close your app and return later without losing their selected items.

### 2. [Checkout](/docs/storefront/orders/checkout)
When the customer is ready to pay, the Cart transitions into the Checkout phase.
- The customer provides their delivery address.
- Storefront communicates with FleetOps to calculate a dynamic delivery fee (a [Service Quote](/docs/fleetops/operations/service-quotes)) based on the distance from the Store to the customer.
- The customer applies any [Promotions](/docs/storefront/promotions) or discount codes.
- The final total (Subtotal + Delivery Fee - Discounts + Taxes) is calculated.

### 3. [Payment & Order Creation](/docs/storefront/orders/payment-gateways)
The customer provides their payment details.
- Storefront securely processes the payment via an integrated [Payment Gateway](/docs/storefront/orders/payment-gateways) (e.g., Stripe).
- Upon successful payment, the Cart is destroyed, and a formal **Order** is created.
- This Order is instantly visible in both the Storefront Console (for the merchant to prepare) and the FleetOps Console (for the dispatcher to assign to a driver).

## Managing Orders in the Console

As a merchant or administrator, you can view and manage all Storefront orders directly from the Fleetbase Console.

1. Navigate to **Storefront** > **Orders**.
2. Here you will see a real-time list of incoming orders.
3. Clicking on an order reveals its full details, including the customer's information, the purchased items, the payment status, and the current delivery status.

### Order Statuses

A Storefront Order shares its status with the underlying FleetOps Order. As the delivery progresses, the status updates automatically:

- **Created**: The order has been paid for and created, but the merchant has not yet accepted it.
- **Preparing**: The merchant has accepted the order and is preparing the items.
- **Dispatched**: A driver has been assigned to pick up the order.
- **In Transit**: The driver has picked up the order and is en route to the customer.
- **Completed**: The driver has successfully delivered the order.
- **Canceled**: The order was canceled by the merchant, the customer, or the dispatcher.

## Integration with FleetOps

It is important to understand that a Storefront Order is essentially a specialized wrapper around a FleetOps Order.

When a Storefront Order is created, FleetOps automatically handles the logistics:
- The **Pickup Address** is set to the Store's [Location](/docs/storefront/stores/store-locations).
- The **Dropoff Address** is set to the customer's provided address.
- The **Payload** is populated with the specific items purchased.

This deep integration means you do not need to build complex webhooks or synchronization logic between your e-commerce backend and your dispatch system — Fleetbase handles it all natively.
