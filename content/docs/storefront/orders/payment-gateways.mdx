# Payment Gateways

To accept digital payments in your Storefront application, you must configure a **Payment Gateway**.

Storefront acts as the orchestrator, securely passing payment intents between your frontend application and the payment provider, ensuring that sensitive credit card data never touches your own servers.

## Supported Gateways

Currently, Storefront officially supports **Stripe** as its primary payment gateway.

Stripe provides a robust, globally available infrastructure for processing credit cards, Apple Pay, Google Pay, and various local payment methods.

*Note: Support for additional gateways (such as PayPal, Square, or regional providers) can be added via custom extensions.*

## Configuring Stripe

To enable Stripe payments in your Storefront:

1. Create a [Stripe account](https://stripe.com) if you don't already have one.
2. In your Stripe Dashboard, locate your **Publishable Key** and **Secret Key**.
3. Navigate to **Storefront** > **Settings** > **Gateways** in the Fleetbase Console.
4. Select **Stripe** from the list of available gateways.
5. Enter your Publishable Key and Secret Key.
6. (Optional) If you are testing your application, ensure you use your Stripe *Test* keys. When you are ready to launch, swap them for your *Live* keys.
7. Click **Save**.

## The Payment Flow

When a customer reaches the [Checkout](/docs/storefront/orders/checkout) phase, the payment flow works as follows:

1. **Create Payment Intent**: Your frontend application sends a request to the [Storefront API](/docs/api-reference/storefront) to initialize a payment for the current Cart total.
2. **Storefront Communicates with Stripe**: Storefront securely contacts Stripe using your Secret Key and creates a `PaymentIntent`.
3. **Return Client Secret**: Storefront returns the `client_secret` (generated by Stripe) to your frontend application.
4. **Collect Payment Details**: Your frontend application uses the Stripe SDK (e.g., Stripe.js or the Stripe React Native SDK) to securely collect the customer's credit card details.
5. **Confirm Payment**: The Stripe SDK sends the credit card details directly to Stripe's servers, along with the `client_secret`.
6. **Webhook Confirmation**: Stripe processes the payment and sends a secure webhook back to Storefront, confirming the transaction was successful.
7. **Order Creation**: Upon receiving the webhook, Storefront finalizes the Cart and creates the formal Order.

This flow ensures PCI compliance, as the raw credit card numbers are handled entirely by Stripe and never pass through the Fleetbase API.

## Saving Payment Methods

If a customer is logged in (a registered user), they can choose to save their payment method for future purchases.

When they do this, Stripe generates a secure token representing the card. Storefront saves this token to the [Customer](/docs/storefront/customers) record.

During future checkouts, the customer can simply select their saved card, allowing for a frictionless, one-click checkout experience.

## Cash on Delivery (COD)

In addition to digital payment gateways, Storefront also supports **Cash on Delivery (COD)**.

If you enable COD in your Storefront settings, customers can choose to pay the driver in cash when the order arrives.

When a COD order is created, its payment status is marked as `pending`. The driver is responsible for collecting the cash and updating the order status to `paid` via the FleetOps driver app upon successful delivery.
