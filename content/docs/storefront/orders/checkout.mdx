# Checkout

The **Checkout** phase is where a customer finalizes their purchase, provides delivery details, and completes payment.

In Storefront, Checkout is not a separate entity from the [Cart](/docs/storefront/orders/cart). Instead, it is a process that transitions the Cart into a finalized Order.

## The Checkout Flow

When a customer is ready to pay, your frontend application should guide them through the following steps:

### 1. Delivery Details
The customer must provide a delivery address.

If the customer is a registered user, they can select from their [Saved Addresses](/docs/storefront/customers). If they are a guest, they must enter a new address.

Your frontend application sends this address to the [Storefront API](/docs/api-reference/storefront).

### 2. Calculating the Delivery Fee
Once Storefront has the delivery address, it communicates with FleetOps to calculate the delivery fee.

FleetOps uses its [Routing & Dispatching](/docs/fleetops/operations/routing-and-dispatching) engine to determine the distance between the Store's [Location](/docs/storefront/stores/store-locations) and the customer's delivery address. It then applies the appropriate [Service Rates](/docs/fleetops/operations/service-rates) to generate a **Service Quote**.

This Service Quote is returned to Storefront and added to the Cart's total as the `delivery_fee`.

### 3. Applying Promotions
If the customer has a discount code, they can enter it now.

Storefront validates the code against active [Promotions](/docs/storefront/promotions). If valid, the discount is applied to the Cart's subtotal.

### 4. Payment
The customer selects a payment method (e.g., Credit Card, Apple Pay, Cash on Delivery).

If they choose a digital payment method, your frontend application securely collects their payment details and processes the transaction via your configured [Payment Gateway](/docs/storefront/orders/payment-gateways).

### 5. Order Creation
Once payment is successful, your frontend application sends a final `POST /v1/checkout` request to the Storefront API.

Storefront performs a final validation:
- Is the Store still open?
- Are all products still active?
- Was the payment successful?

If everything is valid, Storefront destroys the Cart and creates a formal **Order**.

## The Order Object

The newly created Order object contains all the details of the transaction:
- A unique `order_id` (e.g., `ORD-12345`).
- The customer's details.
- The delivery address.
- The purchased items (the Payload).
- The financial breakdown (Subtotal, Delivery Fee, Discount, Taxes, Total).
- The current status (`created`).

Your frontend application should display an Order Confirmation screen to the customer, showing them their receipt and an estimated delivery time.

## Integration with FleetOps

As soon as the Storefront Order is created, it is instantly visible in the FleetOps Console.

The dispatcher (or the automated [Orchestrator](/docs/fleetops/operations/orchestrator)) can now assign a driver to pick up the order from the Store and deliver it to the customer.

As the driver progresses (e.g., "Dispatched", "In Transit", "Completed"), the status of the Storefront Order updates automatically, allowing your frontend application to provide real-time tracking updates to the customer.
