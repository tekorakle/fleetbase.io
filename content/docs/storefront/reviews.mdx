# Reviews & Ratings

Social proof is one of the most powerful drivers of e-commerce conversions. The **Reviews** system in Storefront allows you to collect and display customer feedback on both individual products and entire stores.

## How Reviews Work

In Storefront, a Review consists of:
- A **Rating**: A numerical score, typically from 1 to 5 stars.
- A **Comment**: Optional text feedback provided by the customer.
- A **Subject**: The entity being reviewed (either a `Product` or a `Store`).
- An **Author**: The `Customer` who submitted the review.

## Product Reviews

Product reviews help customers make informed purchasing decisions.

When a customer views a product in your frontend application, you can use the [Storefront API](/docs/api-reference/storefront) to fetch and display the product's average rating and a list of individual reviews.

### Submitting a Product Review

Customers can submit reviews via the API. Typically, you would prompt a customer to leave a review a few days after their order has been delivered.

```json
// POST /v1/reviews
{
  "subject_type": "product",
  "subject_id": "prod_123",
  "rating": 5,
  "content": "Absolutely delicious, arrived hot and fresh!"
}
```

## Store Reviews

In a multi-vendor marketplace (like a food delivery app), customers often want to review the entire restaurant or store, not just a specific item they ordered.

Store reviews function exactly like product reviews, but the `subject_type` is set to `store`.

The average rating of a Store is often displayed prominently in the marketplace listing, helping customers choose where to order from.

## Managing Reviews

As an administrator, you can manage all submitted reviews from the Fleetbase Console.

1. Navigate to **Storefront** > **Reviews**.
2. Here you can see a list of all reviews, filterable by rating, subject, or author.

### Moderation

Unfortunately, not all reviews are helpful or appropriate. Storefront provides basic moderation tools:

- **Approval**: By default, reviews may be published immediately. However, you can configure your frontend to only display reviews that have been explicitly marked as `approved` in the Console.
- **Deletion**: If a review violates your platform's terms of service (e.g., contains offensive language or spam), an administrator can delete it from the Console.

## Calculating Averages

Storefront automatically calculates and caches the average rating for every Product and Store.

When you fetch a Product or Store via the API, the response includes a `rating` attribute (the average score) and a `reviews_count` attribute (the total number of reviews). This ensures your frontend can display this critical information instantly, without needing to fetch and average all the individual reviews on the fly.
