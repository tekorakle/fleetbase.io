# Customers

In Storefront, a **Customer** represents an end-user who interacts with your e-commerce platform or marketplace app.

Managing customers effectively is crucial for providing a seamless checkout experience and building long-term loyalty.

## The Customer Entity

A Customer record in Storefront stores essential information about the user:

- **Basic Details**: Name, Email, Phone Number.
- **Authentication**: Securely hashed passwords or tokens for logging into your frontend application.
- **Saved Addresses**: A list of physical locations the customer frequently uses for delivery (e.g., "Home", "Work").
- **Saved Payment Methods**: Secure tokens representing credit cards or other payment methods, allowing for one-click checkout.
- **Order History**: A complete record of every purchase the customer has made across your [Networks & Stores](/docs/storefront/networks-and-stores).

## Customer Management in the Console

As an administrator, you can view and manage your customer base directly from the Fleetbase Console.

1. Navigate to **Storefront** > **Customers**.
2. Here you will see a list of all registered users.
3. Clicking on a specific customer reveals their full profile, including their order history and saved addresses.

From this interface, you can:
- Update a customer's contact information.
- Manually add or edit a saved delivery address.
- View their past orders to assist with customer support inquiries.
- Delete a customer account (which also removes their saved payment methods and addresses, though historical order records are retained for accounting purposes).

## Guest Checkout vs. Registered Users

Storefront supports both guest checkout and registered user accounts.

### Guest Checkout
If a user completes a purchase without creating an account, Storefront still creates a "Guest" Customer record. This record stores the name, email, and phone number provided during checkout so the order can be processed and tracked.

However, guest customers cannot save addresses or payment methods for future use, and they cannot log in to view their order history.

### Registered Users
When a user creates an account (via the [Authentication](/docs/storefront/customers/authentication) API), they become a fully registered Customer.

Registered users benefit from a faster checkout experience because their addresses and payment methods are securely saved. They can also log in to your frontend application to track active orders, view past receipts, and manage their profile.

## The Customer API

If you are building a custom frontend application, you will use the [Storefront API](/docs/api-reference/storefront) to handle all customer-related actions.

The API provides endpoints for:
- Registering new accounts.
- Logging in and generating authentication tokens.
- Fetching the authenticated customer's profile.
- Adding, updating, and deleting saved addresses.
- Retrieving the customer's order history.

For detailed information on implementing user login and registration, see the [Authentication](/docs/storefront/customers/authentication) guide.
