# Store Locations

A **Store Location** represents the physical address where a Store operates.

This is a critical piece of data because it serves as the **Pickup Address** when an order is routed to FleetOps for delivery.

A single Store can have multiple Locations (e.g., a franchise with five restaurants in the same city). When a customer places an order, Storefront automatically determines which Location is closest to the customer's delivery address and routes the order there.

## Adding a Location

To add a physical location to a Store:

1. Navigate to **Storefront** > **Stores** in the Fleetbase Console.
2. Open the Store you want to manage.
3. Go to the **Locations** tab.
4. Click **Add Location**.
5. Provide the details:
   - **Name**: A descriptive name for the location (e.g., "Downtown Branch", "Warehouse A").
   - **Address**: The full street address. As you type, Fleetbase will attempt to geocode the address and place a pin on the map.
   - **Coordinates**: If the address is not found, you can manually drag the pin on the map or enter the exact Latitude and Longitude.
   - **Contact Info**: A phone number or email address specific to this location.
6. Click **Save**.

## Delivery Zones

By default, a Store Location can deliver anywhere within the maximum radius defined in your global [Routing Settings](/docs/fleetops/settings/routing).

However, you can restrict a Location to only serve specific geographic areas by assigning it to a **Zone**.

Zones are defined in FleetOps (see [Zones](/docs/fleetops/operations/zones)). Once a Zone is created, you can link it to a Store Location.

If a customer tries to place an order for delivery outside of the Location's assigned Zone, Storefront will reject the order and inform the customer that they are outside the delivery area.

## Managing Multiple Locations

If a Store has multiple Locations, Storefront uses a nearest-neighbor algorithm to determine which Location should fulfill an incoming order.

1. The customer enters their delivery address.
2. Storefront calculates the straight-line distance from the delivery address to every active Location belonging to the Store.
3. The order is assigned to the Location with the shortest distance.

If the closest Location is currently closed (based on its [Operating Hours](/docs/storefront/stores/operating-hours)), Storefront will check the next closest Location. If all Locations are closed or out of range, the customer will not be able to complete the checkout.
