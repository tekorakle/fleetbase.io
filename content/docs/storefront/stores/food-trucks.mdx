# Food Trucks

Storefront is uniquely positioned to handle mobile merchants, such as food trucks, pop-up shops, or mobile service providers.

Because Storefront is built on top of FleetOps (a logistics engine), it natively understands that a "Store" doesn't have to be a fixed building.

## How Mobile Stores Work

In a traditional e-commerce system, a store's address is static. In Storefront, a Store's [Location](/docs/storefront/stores/store-locations) can be updated dynamically.

When a food truck moves to a new spot for the day, the owner simply updates their Location in the Fleetbase Console (or via a custom merchant app using the [Storefront API](/docs/api-reference/storefront)).

1. The owner updates the Location's coordinates (Latitude/Longitude) to their current spot.
2. Storefront instantly updates the Store's position in the customer-facing app.
3. Customers browsing the app will now see the food truck if they are within its delivery radius.
4. When an order is placed, FleetOps uses the *new* coordinates as the Pickup Address for the delivery driver.

## Setting Up a Food Truck

To configure a Store as a mobile merchant:

1. Create a [Store](/docs/storefront/stores/stores-and-networks) as normal.
2. Add a single [Location](/docs/storefront/stores/store-locations) to the Store.
3. Instead of entering a fixed street address, you can leave the address blank and rely entirely on the Coordinates (Latitude/Longitude).
4. Ensure the Store's [Operating Hours](/docs/storefront/stores/operating-hours) are updated to reflect when the truck is open for business at that specific location.

## Dynamic Delivery Zones

If a food truck moves across the city, its delivery area needs to move with it.

By default, Storefront uses a simple radius around the Store's current Location (e.g., "Deliver within 5 miles"). This is ideal for mobile merchants, as the delivery zone automatically shifts whenever the Location coordinates are updated.

If you use fixed geographic [Zones](/docs/fleetops/operations/zones) (e.g., "Only deliver to the Downtown zone"), you must remember to update the Store's assigned Zone when it moves to a new neighborhood.

## Real-Time Tracking (Advanced)

For advanced implementations, you can integrate a GPS [Device](/docs/fleetops/connectivity/devices) directly into the food truck.

By linking the GPS device to the Store's Location via the Fleetbase API, the Store's position in your customer app can update in real-time as the truck drives down the street, creating a truly dynamic marketplace experience.
