# Stores & Networks

The foundational entities in Storefront are **Stores** and **Networks**.

Understanding how these two entities relate to each other is crucial for setting up your e-commerce platform correctly, whether you are building a single-brand app or a massive multi-vendor marketplace.

## What is a Store?

A **Store** represents a single, distinct brand or merchant.

Every Store has its own:
- **Catalog**: The specific [Products](/docs/storefront/catalog/products) and [Categories](/docs/storefront/catalog/categories) it sells.
- **Locations**: The physical places where orders are prepared and picked up by drivers.
- **Operating Hours**: When the store is open to accept orders.
- **Branding**: Its own logo, banner image, and description.

If you are building a Direct-to-Consumer (D2C) app for a single restaurant or retail brand, you will only need to create one Store.

## What is a Network?

A **Network** is a collection of multiple Stores.

If you are building a multi-vendor marketplace (like UberEats, DoorDash, or a local farmer's market app), you will create a single Network, and then add multiple independent Stores to that Network.

When a customer opens your marketplace app, they are interacting with the Network. The Network queries all of its associated Stores and displays the ones that are currently open and within delivery range of the customer's location.

## Key Differences

| Feature | Store | Network |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Primary Use Case** | Single brand, merchant, or restaurant. | Multi-vendor marketplace or franchise aggregator. |
| **Catalog** | Owns its own products and categories. | Does not own products directly; aggregates products from its Stores. |
| **Locations** | Has specific physical addresses for pickup. | Does not have physical locations; covers a broad geographic area. |
| **Checkout** | Customers check out from a specific Store. | Customers browse the Network, but ultimately check out from a specific Store within it. |

## Setting Up Your Architecture

### Scenario 1: Single Brand, Single Location
*Example: A local pizza shop with one physical restaurant.*
- Create **1 Store**.
- Add **1 Location** to that Store.
- You do not need a Network.

### Scenario 2: Single Brand, Multiple Locations (Franchise)
*Example: A coffee shop chain with 5 locations across the city.*
- Create **1 Store** (representing the brand).
- Add **5 Locations** to that Store.
- When a customer orders, Storefront automatically routes the order to the Location closest to them.
- You do not need a Network.

### Scenario 3: Multi-Vendor Marketplace
*Example: A food delivery app featuring 50 different local restaurants.*
- Create **1 Network** (representing your marketplace app).
- Create **50 Stores** (one for each restaurant).
- Add each Store to your Network.
- Each Store manages its own Catalog and Locations.
