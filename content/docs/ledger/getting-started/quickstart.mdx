# Quickstart

This guide walks you through reviewing your default chart of accounts, configuring a payment gateway, and creating your first invoice.

## Step 1 — Review Your Chart of Accounts

When your Fleetbase company is created, Ledger automatically provisions a default chart of accounts with standard accounts for assets, liabilities, equity, revenue, and expenses.

<Steps>
  <Step>
    Navigate to **Ledger → Accounting → Accounts**.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Review the default accounts. You will see accounts grouped by type: Assets, Liabilities, Equity, Revenue, Expenses.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Optionally add custom accounts for your business (e.g., a specific revenue account per service type). See [Chart of Accounts](/docs/ledger/accounting/chart-of-accounts).
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Step 2 — Configure a Payment Gateway

<Steps>
  <Step>
    Navigate to **Ledger → Payments → Gateways**.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Click **Add Gateway** and select **Stripe** (or **Cash** for testing).
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Enter your Stripe **Publishable Key** and **Secret Key**. Use test keys during development.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Set **Environment** to `sandbox` while testing.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Click **Save**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Step 3 — Create an Invoice

<Steps>
  <Step>
    Navigate to **Ledger → Billing → Invoices**.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Click **New Invoice**.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Select a **Customer** (driver, contact, or company).
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Set the **Invoice Date** and **Due Date**.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Add one or more **Line Items** — each with a description, quantity, unit price, and optional tax rate.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Click **Save**. The invoice is created in `draft` status. Subtotal, tax, and total are calculated automatically.
  </Step>
</Steps>

![New invoice form — customer, dates, line items, totals](/images/placeholder.png)

## Step 4 — Send the Invoice

<Steps>
  <Step>
    Open the draft invoice.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Click **Mark as Sent** — the status changes to `sent` and the invoice is flagged as dispatched to the customer.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Share the public invoice URL with the customer so they can view and pay it online without logging in.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Step 5 — Record Payment

When the customer pays:

<Steps>
  <Step>
    Open the invoice.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Click **Record Payment**.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Enter the amount received and select the payment method.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Click **Save**. The invoice status updates to `paid` (or `partial` if partial payment). A transaction record is created automatically.
  </Step>
</Steps>
