# Chart of Accounts

The **Chart of Accounts** is the structured list of all accounts used by your organization to record financial transactions. It is the foundation of your accounting system — every financial event is categorized into one of these accounts.

Navigate to **Ledger → Accounting → Accounts**.

![Chart of accounts — accounts grouped by type with codes, names, and balances](/images/placeholder.png)

## Default Accounts

When your Fleetbase company is created, Ledger automatically provisions a default chart of accounts. You will see accounts for:

- **Assets**: Cash, Checking Account, Accounts Receivable, Wallet Asset
- **Liabilities**: Accounts Payable, Wallet Liability
- **Equity**: Retained Earnings, Owner's Capital
- **Revenue**: Sales Revenue, Service Revenue, Interest Income
- **Expenses**: Cost of Goods Sold, Operating Expenses, Bank Fees

System accounts created automatically are marked `is_system_account: true` and cannot be deleted.

## Account Attributes

| Field | Description |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Name** | Descriptive account name (e.g., "Sales Revenue") |
| **Code** | Short identifier for the account (e.g., "4000") |
| **Type** | asset, liability, equity, revenue, or expense |
| **Currency** | ISO 4217 currency code for this account |
| **Status** | active or inactive |
| **Description** | Optional notes about the account's purpose |
| **Balance** | Computed from journal entries; automatically updated |

## Creating an Account

<Steps>
  <Step>
    Navigate to **Ledger → Accounting → Accounts**.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Click **New Account**.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Enter the **Name**, **Code**, and select the **Type**.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Set the **Currency** (defaults to your company currency).
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Click **Save**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Account Codes

Account codes provide a structured namespace for your chart. A common convention:

| Code Range | Type |
| :--- | :--- |
| 1000–1999 | Assets |
| 2000–2999 | Liabilities |
| 3000–3999 | Equity |
| 4000–4999 | Revenue |
| 5000–5999 | Expenses |

Codes are unique per company. Your frontend reports use codes to group and sort accounts in standard financial statement order.

## Balance Calculation

Account balances are computed from the sum of all posted journal entries:

- **Asset & Expense accounts** (debit-normal): `Balance = Total Debits - Total Credits`
- **Liability, Equity & Revenue accounts** (credit-normal): `Balance = Total Credits - Total Debits`

You can force a balance recalculation from the account detail panel by clicking **Recalculate Balance**.

## Viewing the Account Ledger

Click any account in the chart to open its **Account Ledger** — a chronological list of all journal entries for that account with a running balance column. Filter by date range to see activity for a specific period.
