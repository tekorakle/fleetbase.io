# Accounting

The **Accounting** module in Ledger provides the foundational tools for managing your organization's financial records, ensuring compliance, and generating accurate financial reports.

It is designed to handle the complex accounting needs of logistics and e-commerce operations, integrating seamlessly with [FleetOps](/docs/fleetops) and [Storefront](/docs/storefront).

## Core Concepts

### [Chart of Accounts](/docs/ledger/accounting/chart-of-accounts)
The Chart of Accounts is a comprehensive list of all the accounts used by your organization to record financial transactions. It is the foundation of your accounting system, categorizing every transaction into assets, liabilities, equity, revenue, or expenses.

### [General Ledger](/docs/ledger/accounting/general-ledger)
The General Ledger is the master record of all financial transactions across your organization. It aggregates data from all your accounts, providing a complete and accurate picture of your financial health.

### [Journal Entries](/docs/ledger/accounting/journal-entries)
Journal Entries are the individual records of financial transactions. Every time money moves—whether it's a customer payment, a vendor payout, or an internal transfer—a Journal Entry is created to record the event in the General Ledger.

## The Accounting Lifecycle

A typical accounting lifecycle in Fleetbase involves the following steps:

1. **Transaction Creation**: A financial event occurs (e.g., a customer pays for an order in Storefront).
2. **Journal Entry Generation**: Ledger automatically generates a [Journal Entry](/docs/ledger/accounting/journal-entries) to record the transaction.
3. **Account Update**: The Journal Entry updates the balances of the relevant accounts in the [Chart of Accounts](/docs/ledger/accounting/chart-of-accounts).
4. **General Ledger Update**: The updated account balances are reflected in the [General Ledger](/docs/ledger/accounting/general-ledger).
5. **Reporting**: You can generate financial reports (e.g., an Income Statement or Balance Sheet) based on the data in the General Ledger.

## Automated Accounting

While you can manually create Journal Entries, Ledger is designed to automate the accounting process wherever possible.

### FleetOps Integration
When a driver completes an [Order](/docs/fleetops/operations/orders) in FleetOps, the system can automatically generate a Journal Entry to record the delivery fee as an expense and the customer payment as revenue.

### Storefront Integration
When a customer completes a checkout in Storefront, the system automatically generates a Journal Entry to record the sale, updating your revenue and inventory accounts accordingly.
