# Payments & Transactions

The **Payments** module in Ledger provides the infrastructure to process digital payments, track earnings, and manage payouts across your Fleetbase ecosystem.

Whether you are collecting payments from customers in [Storefront](/docs/storefront) or paying drivers for completed deliveries in [FleetOps](/docs/fleetops), Ledger acts as the central financial hub.

## Core Concepts

### [Gateways](/docs/ledger/payments/gateways)
Payment Gateways (like Stripe) are the external services that actually process credit cards and move money between real-world bank accounts. Ledger integrates with these gateways to securely process payments without sensitive data ever touching your servers.

### [Wallets](/docs/ledger/payments/wallets)
Wallets are digital accounts within Fleetbase used to track the financial balance of a specific entity (e.g., a driver, a vendor, a customer, or your own organization).

### [Transactions](/docs/ledger/payments/transactions)
Transactions are the fundamental records of money moving into, out of, or between Wallets. Every financial event—a customer payment, a driver earning, a vendor payout—is recorded as a Transaction.

## The Payment Lifecycle

A typical payment lifecycle in a Fleetbase marketplace involves all three of these concepts working together:

1. **Customer Payment**: A customer purchases an item in Storefront. The [Gateway](/docs/ledger/payments/gateways) processes their credit card.
2. **Transaction Creation**: Ledger receives confirmation from the Gateway and automatically generates a `payment` [Transaction](/docs/ledger/payments/transactions).
3. **Wallet Credit**: The Transaction credits the organization's [Wallet](/docs/ledger/payments/wallets) with the total amount.
4. **Commission Split (Transfer)**: If the item was sold by a third-party vendor, Ledger automatically generates a `transfer` Transaction, moving the vendor's portion of the sale from the organization's Wallet to the vendor's Wallet.
5. **Driver Earning (Credit)**: When a driver delivers the order, FleetOps generates a `credit` Transaction, adding the delivery fee to the driver's Wallet.
6. **Payout (Debit)**: At the end of the week, the vendor and the driver request a payout. Ledger generates `debit` Transactions, reducing their Wallet balances to zero, and uses the Gateway (e.g., Stripe Connect) to transfer the funds to their real-world bank accounts.
