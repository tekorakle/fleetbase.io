# Payment Gateways

To process digital payments—such as credit cards, Apple Pay, or Google Pay—you must configure a **Payment Gateway** in Ledger.

Ledger acts as the central financial hub, securely passing payment intents between your frontend applications (like [Storefront](/docs/storefront)) and the payment provider, ensuring that sensitive credit card data never touches your own servers.

## Supported Gateways

Currently, Ledger officially supports **Stripe** as its primary payment gateway.

Stripe provides a robust, globally available infrastructure for processing payments, issuing refunds, and managing payouts to vendors or drivers.

*Note: Support for additional gateways (such as PayPal, Square, or regional providers) can be added via custom extensions.*

## Configuring Stripe

To enable Stripe payments in Ledger:

1. Create a [Stripe account](https://stripe.com) if you don't already have one.
2. In your Stripe Dashboard, locate your **Publishable Key** and **Secret Key**.
3. Navigate to **Ledger** > **Settings** > **Gateways** in the Fleetbase Console.
4. Select **Stripe** from the list of available gateways.
5. Enter your Publishable Key and Secret Key.
6. (Optional) If you are testing your application, ensure you use your Stripe *Test* keys. When you are ready to launch, swap them for your *Live* keys.
7. Click **Save**.

## The Payment Flow

When a customer makes a purchase (e.g., during [Storefront Checkout](/docs/storefront/orders/checkout)), the payment flow works as follows:

1. **Create Payment Intent**: Your frontend application sends a request to the Fleetbase API to initialize a payment.
2. **Ledger Communicates with Stripe**: Ledger securely contacts Stripe using your Secret Key and creates a `PaymentIntent`.
3. **Return Client Secret**: Ledger returns the `client_secret` (generated by Stripe) to your frontend application.
4. **Collect Payment Details**: Your frontend application uses the Stripe SDK (e.g., Stripe.js or the Stripe React Native SDK) to securely collect the customer's credit card details.
5. **Confirm Payment**: The Stripe SDK sends the credit card details directly to Stripe's servers, along with the `client_secret`.
6. **Webhook Confirmation**: Stripe processes the payment and sends a secure webhook back to Ledger, confirming the transaction was successful.
7. **Transaction Creation**: Upon receiving the webhook, Ledger automatically generates a `payment` [Transaction](/docs/ledger/payments/transactions) and credits the appropriate [Wallet](/docs/ledger/payments/wallets).

This flow ensures PCI compliance, as the raw credit card numbers are handled entirely by Stripe and never pass through the Fleetbase API.

## Saving Payment Methods

If a customer is logged in (a registered user), they can choose to save their payment method for future purchases.

When they do this, Stripe generates a secure token representing the card. Ledger saves this token to the customer's record.

During future checkouts, the customer can simply select their saved card, allowing for a frictionless, one-click checkout experience.

## Payouts (Stripe Connect)

If you operate a multi-vendor marketplace or need to pay independent contractors (drivers), you can use **Stripe Connect** in conjunction with Ledger.

Stripe Connect allows you to route funds directly to your vendors' or drivers' bank accounts.

When you issue a `payout` [Transaction](/docs/ledger/payments/transactions) from a vendor's [Wallet](/docs/ledger/payments/wallets), Ledger communicates with Stripe Connect to initiate the transfer of funds from your platform account to their connected bank account.
