# Transactions

A **Transaction** is the fundamental record of money moving into, out of, or between [Wallets](/docs/ledger/payments/wallets) in Ledger.

Every financial event in your Fleetbase ecosystem—whether it's a customer paying for an order, a driver earning a delivery fee, or a vendor receiving a payout—is recorded as a Transaction.

## Transaction Types

Transactions are categorized by their `type`, which defines the nature of the financial event:

- **`credit`**: Money added to a Wallet (e.g., a customer top-up, a driver's earnings).
- **`debit`**: Money removed from a Wallet (e.g., a payout to a vendor's bank account, a customer spending their store credit).
- **`transfer`**: Money moved from one Wallet to another (e.g., transferring a commission fee from a vendor's Wallet to your organization's Wallet).
- **`payment`**: A specific type of credit representing a successful charge via a [Payment Gateway](/docs/ledger/payments/gateways) (e.g., a Stripe credit card charge).
- **`refund`**: A specific type of debit representing money returned to a customer via a Payment Gateway.

## Viewing Transactions

You can view all financial activity across your organization from the Fleetbase Console:

1. Navigate to **Ledger** > **Transactions**.
2. Here you will see a comprehensive, chronological list of all transactions.
3. You can filter the list by:
   - **Type** (e.g., show only `refunds`).
   - **Status** (e.g., show only `pending` or `failed` transactions).
   - **Wallet** (e.g., show all activity for a specific driver's wallet).
   - **Date Range**.

## Transaction Statuses

Every transaction has a `status` that indicates its current state in the financial lifecycle:

- **`pending`**: The transaction has been initiated but not yet completed (e.g., a bank transfer that takes 3 days to clear).
- **`completed`**: The transaction was successful, and the associated Wallet balances have been updated.
- **`failed`**: The transaction could not be completed (e.g., a declined credit card, insufficient funds for a payout).
- **`canceled`**: The transaction was manually voided before it could complete.

## Automated Transactions

While you can manually create transactions (such as a manual Top-Up or Payout), the vast majority of transactions in Ledger are generated automatically by other Fleetbase modules.

### FleetOps Integration
When a driver completes an [Order](/docs/fleetops/operations/orders) in FleetOps, the system can automatically calculate their earnings based on the [Service Rates](/docs/fleetops/operations/service-rates) and generate a `credit` transaction to their Wallet.

### Storefront Integration
When a customer completes [Checkout](/docs/storefront/orders/checkout) in Storefront, the system automatically generates a `payment` transaction, recording the successful charge from Stripe and crediting the appropriate Wallet.
