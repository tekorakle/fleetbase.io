# Income Statement

The **Income Statement** (also called a Profit & Loss or P&L statement) summarizes your revenue, costs, and expenses during a specific period. It shows whether your organization made a profit or loss.

Navigate to **Ledger → Reports → Income Statement**.

![Income statement — revenue section, expense section, net income row with period label](/images/placeholder.png)

## Report Structure

### Revenue

All **revenue** accounts from your chart of accounts:
- Sales Revenue
- Service Revenue
- Interest Income
- Other revenue

**Total Revenue**

### Expenses

All **expense** accounts:
- Cost of Goods Sold
- Operating Expenses
- Wages and Salaries
- Bank Fees
- Other expenses

**Total Expenses**

### Net Income

```
Net Income = Total Revenue - Total Expenses
```

A positive net income indicates profit; negative indicates a loss for the period.

## Setting the Report Period

1. Navigate to **Ledger → Reports → Income Statement**
2. Set the **Start Date** and **End Date** for the period
3. Click **Generate**

Common periods: current month, current quarter, year-to-date (YTD), last fiscal year.

## Revenue Recognition

Ledger uses **accrual accounting** — revenue is recognized when an invoice is created, not when cash is received. This means:

- An invoice created in March is counted as March revenue, even if payment arrives in April
- When a Fleet-Ops order generates an invoice, revenue appears in the period the order was completed

This is the standard accounting treatment. If you need cash-basis reporting (revenue only when cash is received), filter transactions by the `payment` type instead of using the income statement.

## Comparing Periods

The Ledger dashboard shows year-over-year comparison for total revenue, total expenses, and net income. For detailed period-over-period comparison, generate two income statements for the respective periods and compare them side by side.
