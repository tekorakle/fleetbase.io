# Cash Flow Statement

The **Cash Flow Statement** tracks the actual movement of cash into and out of your organization over a period. Unlike the income statement (which uses accrual accounting), the cash flow statement shows real cash received and paid.

Navigate to **Ledger → Reports → Cash Flow**.

![Cash flow statement — operating, financing, and investing sections with net change in cash](/images/placeholder.png)

## Why Cash Flow Matters

A profitable business on paper can still run out of cash. The cash flow statement tells you whether your operations are generating or consuming cash — critical for liquidity management and planning payroll, vendor payments, and driver payouts.

## Report Structure

### Operating Activities

Cash flows from your core business operations:
- Cash received from customers (invoice payments)
- Cash paid to vendors and suppliers
- Driver earning payouts
- Operating expenses paid

**Net Cash from Operating Activities**

### Financing Activities

Cash flows from financing:
- Wallet top-ups by customers (inflow)
- Driver payouts to bank accounts (outflow)
- Loan repayments (outflow)
- Capital contributions (inflow)

**Net Cash from Financing Activities**

### Investing Activities

Cash flows from investments:
- Purchase of equipment or assets (outflow)
- Proceeds from asset sales (inflow)

**Net Cash from Investing Activities**

### Net Change in Cash

```
Net Change = Operating + Financing + Investing
```

This shows whether cash increased or decreased during the period. Add this to the opening cash balance to get the closing cash balance.

## Setting the Report Period

1. Navigate to **Ledger → Reports → Cash Flow**
2. Set the **Start Date** and **End Date**
3. Click **Generate**

The report draws from wallet transactions and journal entries classified by activity type.
