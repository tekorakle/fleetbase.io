# Financial Reports

The **Reports** module in Ledger provides a suite of standard financial reports to help you understand the health of your business, track revenue, and manage cash flow.

These reports are generated dynamically based on the data in your [General Ledger](/docs/ledger/accounting/general-ledger) and [Wallets](/docs/ledger/payments/wallets).

## Available Reports

### [Income Statement (P&L)](/docs/ledger/reports/income-statement)
Also known as a Profit and Loss statement, this report summarizes your organization's revenues, costs, and expenses during a specific period. It shows your net profit or loss.

### [Balance Sheet](/docs/ledger/reports/balance-sheet)
A snapshot of your organization's financial position at a specific point in time. It details your assets, liabilities, and equity.

### [Cash Flow Statement](/docs/ledger/reports/cash-flow)
This report tracks the flow of cash into and out of your organization over a specific period. It helps you understand how well your business generates cash to pay its debt obligations and fund its operating expenses.

### [Trial Balance](/docs/ledger/reports/trial-balance)
A list of all your general ledger accounts and their balances at a specific point in time. It is used to ensure that total debits equal total credits.

### [A/R Aging](/docs/ledger/reports/ar-aging)
The Accounts Receivable Aging report categorizes your unpaid [Invoices](/docs/ledger/billing/invoices) based on how long they have been outstanding. It helps you identify overdue payments and manage collections.

### [Wallet Summary](/docs/ledger/reports/wallet-summary)
A detailed breakdown of the balances and transaction history for all [Wallets](/docs/ledger/payments/wallets) in your ecosystem. This is particularly useful for tracking driver earnings and vendor payouts.

## Generating Reports

To generate a report:

1. Navigate to **Ledger** > **Reports** in the Fleetbase Console.
2. Select the specific report you want to generate.
3. Define the parameters for the report (e.g., the date range, the specific accounts or wallets to include).
4. Click **Generate**.

## Exporting Reports

Once a report is generated, you can export it in various formats (e.g., CSV, PDF, Excel) for further analysis or to share with your accounting team or external auditors.
