# Vehicles

Vehicles are the physical assets that make up your fleet. In FleetOps, a Vehicle record stores all the essential details about a specific truck, van, car, or bike, including its specifications, current status, and assignment history.

## Adding a Vehicle

To register a new vehicle in your fleet:

1. Navigate to **FleetOps** > **Vehicles** in the Fleetbase Console.
2. Click the **New Vehicle** button.
3. Fill in the vehicle's details:
   - **Make & Model**: The manufacturer and model of the vehicle (e.g., "Ford Transit").
   - **Year**: The manufacturing year.
   - **License Plate**: The vehicle's registration number.
   - **VIN**: The Vehicle Identification Number (optional but recommended).
   - **Internal ID**: An optional custom identifier (e.g., "Truck 04").
   - **Status**: Set the initial status (e.g., `Active`, `Maintenance`).
4. Click **Save**.

Once created, the vehicle is added to your fleet inventory and can be assigned to a driver.

## Managing Vehicle Profiles

Clicking on a vehicle in the list opens its detailed profile, where you can manage its specifications, assignments, and maintenance records.

### Vehicle Specifications

Accurate vehicle specifications are crucial for automated routing and dispatching. FleetOps uses this data to ensure that an order is assigned to a vehicle capable of handling it.

In the vehicle's profile, you can define:
- **Dimensions**: Length, width, and height.
- **Weight Capacity**: The maximum payload the vehicle can carry.
- **Volume Capacity**: The total cargo space available.

If an order requires a specific vehicle type (e.g., a refrigerated truck) or exceeds the weight capacity of a standard van, FleetOps will automatically filter out incompatible vehicles during the dispatch process.

### Assigning a Driver

A vehicle must be assigned to a driver to execute deliveries.

1. In the vehicle's profile, locate the **Driver** section.
2. Click **Assign Driver**.
3. Select an available driver from your workforce.
4. Click **Save**.

The vehicle's current location and status will now be associated with that driver.

### Managing Documents

Just like drivers, vehicles require compliance documentation. You can upload and track important documents directly on the vehicle's profile.

1. In the vehicle's profile, navigate to the **Documents** tab.
2. Click **Upload Document**.
3. Select the file (e.g., a scan of the vehicle's registration or insurance certificate).
4. Provide a name and optional expiration date for the document.

If a document has an expiration date, FleetOps can be configured to send automated [Notifications](/docs/platform/features/notifications) before it expires.

## Vehicle Status and Availability

A vehicle's status indicates its current operational state. This status is updated automatically based on its assignment and activity, but can also be changed manually by a dispatcher or fleet manager.

Common statuses include:
- **Active**: The vehicle is currently assigned to a driver and available for dispatch.
- **Inactive**: The vehicle is not currently assigned or in use.
- **Maintenance**: The vehicle is out of service for repairs or routine maintenance.

### Tracking Location

When a vehicle is `Active` and assigned to a driver who is `Online`, the Navigator app continuously transmits the vehicle's GPS location back to FleetOps. This allows dispatchers to see the vehicle's real-time position on the map and calculate accurate ETAs for active orders.

## Maintenance and Issues

Keeping your fleet on the road is critical. FleetOps provides tools for tracking vehicle maintenance and resolving operational issues.

- **Issues**: Drivers can report vehicle defects (like a flat tire or a check engine light) directly from the Navigator app. These reports are logged as [Issues](/docs/fleetops/management/issues) and associated with the vehicle's profile.
- **Fuel Reports**: Drivers can submit fuel receipts via the app, allowing you to track fuel consumption and expenses per vehicle. These reports are logged as [Fuel Reports](/docs/fleetops/management/fuel-reports).

By monitoring these records, you can identify recurring problems, schedule preventative maintenance, and optimize your fleet's performance.
