# Issues

In logistics, things don't always go according to plan. Vehicles break down, traffic causes delays, and customers aren't always available to receive their deliveries.

The **Issues** system in FleetOps provides a centralized way to log, track, and resolve these operational problems, ensuring that nothing falls through the cracks.

## Types of Issues

Issues can be associated with almost any resource in FleetOps, but they are most commonly linked to:

- **Vehicles**: Mechanical breakdowns, flat tires, or scheduled maintenance.
- **Drivers**: Illness, accidents, or compliance problems (e.g., an expired license).
- **Orders**: Delayed pickups, damaged goods, or customer complaints.

## Reporting an Issue

Issues can be reported manually by dispatchers in the Fleetbase Console, or directly by drivers using the Navigator app.

### Reporting via the Navigator App

When a driver encounters a problem on the road, they can report it immediately from their mobile device:

1. In the Navigator app, the driver taps the **Report Issue** button.
2. They select the category of the issue (e.g., "Vehicle Breakdown", "Traffic Delay").
3. They provide a brief description of the problem.
4. (Optional) They can attach a photo (e.g., a picture of a flat tire or a damaged package).
5. They submit the report.

The issue is instantly logged in FleetOps, and dispatchers can be alerted via [Notifications](/docs/platform/features/notifications).

### Reporting via the Console

Dispatchers and managers can also log issues manually:

1. Navigate to **FleetOps** > **Issues** in the Fleetbase Console.
2. Click the **New Issue** button.
3. Select the **Category** and provide a **Description**.
4. Link the issue to the relevant resource (e.g., select the specific Vehicle or Order).
5. Assign the issue to a specific user (e.g., a fleet manager or a mechanic) for resolution.
6. Click **Save**.

## Tracking and Resolution

Once an issue is logged, it enters a workflow where it can be tracked through to resolution.

### Issue Statuses

Issues typically move through the following statuses:
- **Open**: The issue has been reported but not yet addressed.
- **In Progress**: Someone is actively working on resolving the issue.
- **Resolved**: The problem has been fixed.
- **Closed**: The issue has been reviewed and closed by a manager.

### Collaboration

The built-in [Chat & Collaboration](/docs/platform/features/chat-and-collaboration) system is deeply integrated with Issues.

When viewing an issue, you can use the **Comments** tab to communicate with the driver who reported it, or with the mechanic assigned to fix it. This keeps all communication related to the problem in one place, rather than scattered across emails or text messages.

## Analytics and Reporting

Tracking issues is not just about fixing immediate problems; it's also about identifying long-term trends.

By categorizing and logging every issue, you can use the [Analytics](/docs/fleetops/analytics) tools to answer questions like:
- Which vehicles break down most frequently?
- Are certain drivers involved in more accidents than others?
- What are the most common causes of delayed deliveries?

This data allows you to make informed decisions about preventative maintenance, driver training, and operational improvements.
