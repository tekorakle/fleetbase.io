# Orders

An **Order** is the fundamental unit of work in FleetOps. It represents a request to move goods from one location to another. Every delivery, pickup, or service call your fleet executes is tracked as an Order.

## Creating an Order

Orders can be created manually in the Fleetbase Console, or automatically via the [Core API](/docs/api-reference/core/orders) or an integrated extension like [Storefront](/docs/storefront).

To create an order manually:

1. Navigate to **FleetOps** > **Orders** in the Fleetbase Console.
2. Click the **New Order** button.
3. Fill in the required details:
   - **Pickup**: The starting location. You can select a saved [Place](/docs/fleetops/management/places) or enter a new address.
   - **Dropoff**: The destination location.
   - **Payload**: The items being transported (optional, but recommended for accurate routing and pricing).
   - **Customer**: The [Contact](/docs/fleetops/management/contacts) paying for the service.
   - **Facilitator**: The Contact managing the pickup/dropoff.
4. (Optional) Assign the order to a specific Driver, Vehicle, or Fleet.
5. Click **Save**.

Once created, the order enters the `created` state and is ready for dispatch.

## The Order Lifecycle

An order moves through a series of statuses as it is executed. This lifecycle provides real-time visibility to dispatchers and customers.

The standard order lifecycle is:

1. **Created**: The order exists in the system but has not been assigned.
2. **Dispatched**: The order has been assigned to a driver, and they have been notified via the Navigator app.
3. **Active**: The driver has accepted the order and is en route to the pickup location.
4. **Completed**: The driver has successfully delivered the payload and captured proof of delivery.

### Exception Statuses

Things don't always go perfectly. An order can also enter exception statuses:
- **Canceled**: The order was canceled before completion.
- **Failed**: The driver attempted delivery but was unsuccessful (e.g., the customer was not home).

## Dispatching an Order

Dispatching is the process of assigning an order to a driver and notifying them to begin work.

1. Open the order in the Fleetbase Console.
2. In the **Assignment** section, select a Driver or a Fleet.
3. Click **Dispatch**.

FleetOps will instantly send a push notification to the assigned driver's Navigator app. The driver can then review the order details and tap "Accept" to begin the job.

For more advanced dispatching workflows, including automated assignment, see the [Routing & Dispatching](/docs/fleetops/operations/routing-and-dispatching) documentation.

## Tracking and Proof of Delivery

Once an order is `Active`, dispatchers can track its progress in real-time on the FleetOps map. The driver's GPS location is continuously updated, and FleetOps calculates an Estimated Time of Arrival (ETA) based on current traffic conditions.

When the driver arrives at the dropoff location, they use the Navigator app to complete the order. This process often involves capturing **Proof of Delivery (POD)**.

FleetOps supports several types of POD:
- **Signatures**: The customer signs directly on the driver's mobile device.
- **Photos**: The driver takes a picture of the delivered package (e.g., left on the porch).
- **Notes**: The driver leaves a text note confirming the delivery details.

These POD records are instantly synced back to the Fleetbase Console and permanently attached to the order record.
