# Routing & Dispatching

Routing and dispatching are the core functions of any Transport Management System (TMS). FleetOps provides a flexible set of tools to handle everything from simple manual assignments to complex, algorithm-driven automated routing.

## Manual Dispatching

For smaller fleets or specialized deliveries, manual dispatching gives you complete control over who handles which order.

### The Dispatch Board

The primary interface for manual dispatching is the **Dispatch Board**. This view combines a list of unassigned orders with a real-time map showing the current locations of all your active drivers and vehicles.

To manually dispatch an order:

1. Open the Dispatch Board in the Fleetbase Console.
2. Select an unassigned order from the list.
3. Review the order details (pickup, dropoff, payload requirements).
4. Look at the map to identify the closest available driver with the appropriate vehicle capacity.
5. Drag and drop the order onto the driver, or select the driver from the assignment dropdown.
6. Click **Dispatch**.

The driver will instantly receive a push notification on their Navigator app with the order details.

## Automated Routing

As your order volume grows, manual dispatching becomes inefficient. FleetOps includes a powerful automated routing engine that can assign orders and optimize routes without human intervention.

### How Automated Routing Works

When you enable automated routing, FleetOps uses a combination of algorithms and real-time data to make dispatch decisions. The engine considers:

- **Distance and Traffic**: Calculating the fastest route between the driver's current location, the pickup point, and the dropoff point.
- **Vehicle Capacity**: Ensuring the assigned vehicle has enough space and weight capacity to handle the payload.
- **Driver Schedules**: Checking that the driver is on shift and has enough time remaining to complete the delivery.
- **Service Areas**: Ensuring the driver is authorized to operate in the zones where the pickup and dropoff are located.

### Configuring Automated Routing

Automated routing is highly configurable. You can set rules that dictate how the engine behaves:

- **Batching**: Grouping multiple orders that are close together into a single route for one driver.
- **Prioritization**: Giving preference to certain types of orders (e.g., "Express" deliveries) or specific customers.
- **Auto-Dispatch**: Automatically sending the dispatch notification to the driver as soon as the route is calculated, or holding it for manual review by a dispatcher.

## Route Optimization

Whether you are dispatching manually or automatically, FleetOps provides tools to optimize the routes your drivers take.

### Multi-Stop Routes

For drivers handling multiple deliveries in a single shift, the order in which they visit the stops is critical. FleetOps can automatically sequence a list of waypoints to minimize total driving time and distance.

This optimization takes into account real-time traffic conditions, road closures, and vehicle restrictions (e.g., avoiding low bridges for tall trucks).

### Re-Routing on the Fly

Logistics is dynamic. If a driver encounters an unexpected delay, or if a new urgent order comes in, FleetOps can recalculate the route on the fly.

When a route is updated, the changes are instantly pushed to the driver's Navigator app, ensuring they always have the most efficient path to their next destination.

## Integrated Vendors

If your internal fleet is at capacity, FleetOps allows you to seamlessly overflow orders to third-party delivery networks (like Lalamove or Stuart).

When you assign an order to an [Integrated Vendor](/docs/fleetops/connectivity/integrated-vendors), FleetOps automatically sends the order details to their API and tracks the delivery progress just as if it were being handled by one of your own drivers. This provides a unified dispatching experience, regardless of who is actually executing the delivery.
