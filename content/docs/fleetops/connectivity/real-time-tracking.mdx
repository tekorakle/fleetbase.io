# Real-Time Tracking

The culmination of your [Connectivity](/docs/fleetops/connectivity) efforts is the **Live Map**.

FleetOps aggregates location data from every available source—the Navigator mobile app, dedicated [Devices](/docs/fleetops/connectivity/devices), and third-party [Telematics](/docs/fleetops/connectivity/telematics) integrations—to provide a unified, real-time view of your entire logistics network.

## The Live Map Interface

The Live Map is the central command center for dispatchers. It provides a bird's-eye view of:

- **Active Drivers**: See the current location, speed, and heading of every driver currently on shift.
- **Vehicles**: Monitor the location of your fleet assets, even when they are not actively assigned to an order.
- **Active Orders**: Track the progress of deliveries currently in transit.
- **Unassigned Orders**: Visualize where new orders are originating to make smarter dispatch decisions.

## How Tracking Works

Fleetbase uses a high-performance, event-driven architecture to ensure the map is always up-to-date.

1. **Data Ingestion**: Location updates are received by the Fleetbase API from various sources (Navigator app, webhooks from devices, or polling from telematics providers).
2. **State Update**: The system updates the current location of the corresponding Driver or Vehicle record in the database.
3. **Socket Broadcast**: The new location data is instantly broadcast via [Socket Events](/docs/platform/developer-console/socket-events) to any active Fleetbase Console sessions.
4. **Map Render**: The Live Map receives the socket event and smoothly animates the marker to its new position.

This entire process typically takes less than a second, providing true real-time visibility.

## Tracking Accuracy and Frequency

The accuracy and frequency of the tracking data depend entirely on the source:

- **Navigator App**: When a driver is actively navigating to a destination, the app sends high-frequency updates (often every few seconds) to provide smooth tracking. To preserve battery life, the update frequency decreases when the app is in the background or the driver is stationary.
- **Dedicated Devices**: Hardwired GPS trackers typically provide the most reliable and frequent updates, often configurable (e.g., every 10 seconds while moving, every 5 minutes while parked).
- **Telematics Integrations**: The update frequency depends on the provider's API limits and your polling configuration.

## Customer Tracking Links

Real-time tracking isn't just for dispatchers. You can also share this visibility with your customers.

When an order is dispatched, FleetOps can generate a unique, secure tracking link. This link can be sent to the customer via SMS or email (using [Notifications](/docs/platform/features/notifications)).

When the customer clicks the link, they see a simplified, mobile-friendly map showing:
- The driver's current location and route.
- An Estimated Time of Arrival (ETA).
- The driver's name and vehicle details (optional).
- The current status of the order.

This transparency significantly reduces "Where is my order?" (WISMO) support calls and improves the overall customer experience.
