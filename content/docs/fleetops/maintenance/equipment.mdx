# Equipment

In FleetOps, **Equipment** refers to physical assets that are essential to your operations but are not vehicles themselves.

This can include:
- **Shop Equipment**: Diagnostic scanners, hydraulic lifts, air compressors, or specialized tools used by your mechanics.
- **Cargo Handling Equipment**: Forklifts, pallet jacks, or specialized dollies used in your warehouse or carried on your trucks.
- **Trailers/Containers**: Non-motorized assets that are towed by your vehicles.

Tracking these assets is just as important as tracking your vehicles, as their failure can cause significant operational delays.

## Managing Equipment

To add a new piece of equipment to FleetOps:

1. Navigate to **FleetOps** > **Equipment** in the Fleetbase Console.
2. Click the **New Equipment** button.
3. Provide the details:
   - **Name**: A descriptive name (e.g., "Forklift 02 - Main Warehouse").
   - **Type**: The category of equipment (e.g., `Material Handling`, `Diagnostic Tool`).
   - **Make/Model/Serial Number**: Identifying information for the asset.
   - **Status**: The current operational status (e.g., `Active`, `Maintenance`, `Out of Service`).
4. Click **Save**.

## Equipment Maintenance

Just like vehicles, equipment requires regular maintenance to function safely and efficiently.

FleetOps allows you to manage the maintenance lifecycle of your equipment using the same tools you use for your vehicles:

- **Maintenance Schedules**: You can create a [Maintenance Schedule](/docs/fleetops/maintenance/maintenance-schedules) specifically for a piece of equipment. For example, you might schedule a safety inspection for a forklift every 6 months, or an oil change for a generator every 500 engine hours.
- **Work Orders**: When equipment breaks down or requires scheduled service, you create a [Work Order](/docs/fleetops/maintenance/work-orders) for it. This tracks the repair details, the assigned mechanic, and the cost of any [Parts](/docs/fleetops/maintenance/parts) or labor required.

By tracking equipment maintenance in FleetOps, you ensure that all your operational assets are safe, reliable, and accounted for in your total maintenance budget.
