# Fuel Reports

Fuel is often the largest variable expense in a logistics operation. Tracking fuel consumption accurately is essential for calculating the true cost of your deliveries, identifying inefficient vehicles, and preventing fuel theft.

The **Fuel Reports** system in FleetOps provides a simple, structured way for drivers to log their fuel purchases, which are then aggregated and analyzed in the Fleetbase Console.

## Submitting a Fuel Report

Fuel reports are typically submitted by drivers while they are on the road, using the Navigator mobile app.

### Reporting via the Navigator App

When a driver stops to refuel, they can quickly log the transaction:

1. In the Navigator app, the driver taps the **Fuel Report** button.
2. They enter the **Amount** of fuel purchased (in liters or gallons, depending on your region).
3. They enter the **Total Cost** of the transaction.
4. They enter the vehicle's current **Odometer Reading** (mileage).
5. (Optional) They can take a photo of the fuel receipt and attach it to the report.
6. They submit the report.

The report is instantly synced to FleetOps, where it is associated with both the driver and the vehicle they are currently assigned to.

### Reporting via the Console

Fleet managers can also enter fuel reports manually in the Fleetbase Console. This is useful if you use a corporate fuel card system and receive a consolidated monthly invoice that needs to be entered into the system.

1. Navigate to **FleetOps** > **Fuel Reports** in the Fleetbase Console.
2. Click the **New Fuel Report** button.
3. Select the **Vehicle** and the **Driver**.
4. Enter the **Amount**, **Cost**, and **Odometer Reading**.
5. (Optional) Upload a scan of the receipt.
6. Click **Save**.

## Analyzing Fuel Data

Once fuel reports are logged, FleetOps uses the data to calculate key performance indicators (KPIs) for your fleet.

By comparing the fuel consumed against the distance traveled (calculated from the odometer readings or the GPS tracking data), FleetOps can determine the fuel efficiency of each vehicle.

### Key Metrics

In the [Analytics](/docs/fleetops/analytics) section, you can view reports that highlight:
- **Total Fuel Cost**: The overall spend on fuel across the entire fleet or specific regions.
- **Cost per Mile/Kilometer**: The average fuel cost to operate a vehicle for a single unit of distance.
- **Fuel Efficiency (MPG or L/100km)**: How efficiently each vehicle is using fuel.

### Identifying Inefficiencies

These metrics are invaluable for fleet management. By regularly reviewing fuel reports, you can:
- Identify vehicles that are performing poorly and may require maintenance (e.g., a sudden drop in MPG could indicate a mechanical issue).
- Compare the efficiency of different vehicle makes and models to inform future purchasing decisions.
- Detect potential fuel theft or unauthorized personal use of company vehicles (e.g., if the reported fuel consumption significantly exceeds the expected consumption based on the vehicle's tracked mileage).
