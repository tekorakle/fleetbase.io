# Contacts

In FleetOps, a **Contact** represents any individual or organization that interacts with your logistics network, but is not a direct employee (like a Driver) or a third-party logistics provider (like a Vendor).

Contacts are primarily used to represent:
- **Customers**: The individuals or businesses who place orders with your company.
- **Recipients**: The individuals who receive the deliveries.
- **Facility Managers**: The people responsible for specific pickup or dropoff locations (e.g., a warehouse manager).

## Creating a Contact

To add a new contact to your database:

1. Navigate to **FleetOps** > **Contacts** in the Fleetbase Console.
2. Click the **New Contact** button.
3. Fill in the contact's details:
   - **Name**: The individual's or company's name.
   - **Email**: Essential for sending automated notifications (like order confirmations or receipts).
   - **Phone Number**: Essential for SMS notifications and driver communication.
   - **Internal ID**: An optional custom identifier (e.g., a customer ID from your CRM).
4. Click **Save**.

## Using Contacts in Orders

The primary use of Contacts is within the Order creation process.

When creating an order, you can assign a Contact as the **Customer** (the entity paying for the service) and/or the **Facilitator** (the person managing the pickup or dropoff).

By associating a Contact with an order, FleetOps can automatically:
- Send email or SMS [Notifications](/docs/platform/features/notifications) to the contact when the order status changes (e.g., "Your order is out for delivery").
- Provide the driver with the contact's phone number in the Navigator app, allowing them to call or text the recipient if they have trouble finding the location.

## Managing Contact Profiles

Clicking on a contact in the list opens their detailed profile.

From the profile, you can view the contact's complete history with your organization, including:
- **Recent Orders**: A list of all orders where this contact was the customer or recipient.
- **Saved Places**: A list of addresses frequently associated with this contact (e.g., their home or office address).

This historical data is invaluable for customer support teams, allowing them to quickly pull up a customer's past deliveries and address any issues.
