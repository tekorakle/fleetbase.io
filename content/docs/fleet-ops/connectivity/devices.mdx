# Devices

In FleetOps, a **Device** represents a physical piece of hardware installed in a vehicle or carried by a driver (other than their smartphone running the Navigator app).

Connecting dedicated devices to Fleetbase provides a more reliable, higher-resolution stream of data than relying solely on mobile apps.

## Types of Devices

Fleetbase can ingest data from a wide variety of hardware:

- **GPS Trackers**: Hardwired or battery-powered units that provide continuous, high-frequency location updates, even when the vehicle is turned off.
- **OBD-II Dongles**: Devices that plug into a vehicle's diagnostic port to read engine data, fuel levels, and fault codes.
- **Dashcams**: Internet-connected cameras that can upload video clips of harsh driving events or accidents.
- **Electronic Logging Devices (ELDs)**: Specialized hardware required in many jurisdictions to track driver Hours of Service (HOS) for compliance.

## Registering a Device

Before a device can send data to Fleetbase, it must be registered in the system.

1. Navigate to **FleetOps** > **Devices** in the Fleetbase Console.
2. Click the **New Device** button.
3. Fill in the device details:
   - **Name**: A descriptive name (e.g., "Tracker - Truck 04").
   - **Provider/Manufacturer**: The brand or network the device operates on (e.g., "Teltonika", "CalAmp").
   - **Device ID / IMEI**: The unique identifier for the hardware. This is critical, as it's how Fleetbase matches incoming data to the correct record.
   - **Status**: Set the initial status (e.g., `Active`, `Inactive`).
4. Click **Save**.

## Assigning a Device to a Vehicle

A device on its own only tells you where the *device* is. To make the data useful, you must assign the device to a specific [Vehicle](/docs/fleetops/management/vehicles).

1. Open the device's detailed profile.
2. In the **Assignment** section, select the vehicle the device is installed in.
3. Click **Save**.

Once assigned, any data received from the device (location, speed, engine diagnostics) will automatically update the corresponding vehicle's record in FleetOps.

## Ingesting Device Data

Fleetbase is hardware-agnostic. It does not dictate *how* the device connects to the internet, only how the data is received by the platform.

There are two primary ways device data enters Fleetbase:

### 1. Direct API Integration (Webhooks)
Many modern IoT devices and tracking platforms can be configured to send HTTP POST requests (webhooks) to a specific URL whenever they have new data.

You can configure your device provider to send these webhooks directly to the Fleetbase [Core API](/docs/api-reference/core). You will need to write a small translation layer (often using a serverless function or a Fleetbase Extension) to map the provider's specific JSON payload into the standard Fleetbase format.

### 2. Third-Party Telematics Integration
If your devices are managed by a comprehensive telematics platform (like Samsara or Geotab), you don't need to integrate the individual devices. Instead, you integrate the entire platform. See the [Telematics](/docs/fleetops/connectivity/telematics) documentation for more details.

## Monitoring Device Health

It's critical to know if a device stops reporting data.

In the Devices list, FleetOps displays the **Last Seen** timestamp for every registered device. If a device hasn't reported in for an extended period, it may indicate a dead battery, a broken connection, or tampering.

You can use the [Orchestrator](/docs/fleetops/operations/orchestrator) to set up automated alerts (e.g., "If a device's Last Seen is > 24 hours, send an email to the fleet manager").
