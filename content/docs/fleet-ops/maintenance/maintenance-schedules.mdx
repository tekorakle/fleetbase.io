# Maintenance Schedules

Preventative maintenance is the key to maximizing the lifespan of your fleet and avoiding costly, unexpected breakdowns.

**Maintenance Schedules** in FleetOps allow you to define recurring service intervals for your vehicles based on time, usage (mileage), or engine hours.

## How Schedules Work

A Maintenance Schedule acts as a template. You define the rules for when service is required, and then apply that schedule to one or more vehicles.

FleetOps continuously monitors the vehicles' data (odometer readings from the Navigator app, or engine hours from [Telematics](/docs/fleetops/connectivity/telematics) integrations). When a vehicle approaches the threshold defined in the schedule, the system automatically generates an alert and creates a pending [Work Order](/docs/fleetops/maintenance/work-orders).

## Creating a Maintenance Schedule

To set up a new preventative maintenance routine:

1. Navigate to **FleetOps** > **Maintenance Schedules** in the Fleetbase Console.
2. Click the **New Schedule** button.
3. Provide a **Name** (e.g., "Standard Oil Change - Diesel Trucks").
4. Define the **Service Tasks** that need to be performed (e.g., "Replace oil filter", "Check tire pressure").
5. Set the **Intervals** (detailed below).
6. Click **Save**.

## Defining Intervals

You can trigger maintenance based on three types of intervals. You can use one, or combine them (e.g., "Every 10,000 miles OR every 6 months, whichever comes first").

### 1. Usage (Mileage) Intervals
The most common method for vehicle maintenance.
- *Example*: Every 10,000 miles.
- *How it works*: FleetOps tracks the vehicle's odometer. When the current odometer reading is 10,000 miles higher than the reading at the last completed service, the schedule triggers.

### 2. Time Intervals
Useful for vehicles that aren't driven frequently, or for seasonal maintenance (like winterizing).
- *Example*: Every 6 months.
- *How it works*: FleetOps triggers the schedule exactly 6 months after the date of the last completed service.

### 3. Engine Hour Intervals
Common for heavy machinery, generators, or specialized equipment where mileage isn't an accurate measure of wear and tear.
- *Example*: Every 500 engine hours.
- *How it works*: Requires a [Telematics](/docs/fleetops/connectivity/telematics) integration or an OBD-II [Device](/docs/fleetops/connectivity/devices) that reports engine run time.

## Applying Schedules to Vehicles

Once a schedule is created, you must assign it to the relevant vehicles.

1. Open the Maintenance Schedule.
2. In the **Assigned Vehicles** section, select the vehicles that should follow this routine.
3. Click **Save**.

You can assign multiple schedules to a single vehicle (e.g., a "Monthly Inspection" schedule and an "Annual Major Service" schedule).

## Alerts and Work Orders

When a vehicle approaches a scheduled service interval, FleetOps changes the vehicle's maintenance status to `Due Soon`.

You can configure how much advance warning you want (e.g., "Alert me 500 miles before the service is due"). This gives your fleet manager time to order necessary [Parts](/docs/fleetops/maintenance/parts) and schedule the downtime.

When the interval is reached, the status changes to `Overdue`.

At any point during the `Due Soon` or `Overdue` phases, a fleet manager can click a button to instantly generate a [Work Order](/docs/fleetops/maintenance/work-orders) pre-populated with the tasks defined in the schedule. Once that Work Order is marked as completed, the schedule's interval resets, and the countdown begins again.
