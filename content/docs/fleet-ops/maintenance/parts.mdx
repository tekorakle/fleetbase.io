# Parts

If you operate your own maintenance shop, tracking your inventory of spare parts is essential for controlling costs and ensuring mechanics have what they need to complete repairs quickly.

The **Parts** section of FleetOps provides a simple inventory management system specifically designed for vehicle maintenance.

## Managing the Parts Catalog

A Part in FleetOps represents a specific type of component (e.g., "Oil Filter - Model X", "Brake Pad Set - Heavy Duty").

To add a new part to your catalog:

1. Navigate to **FleetOps** > **Parts** in the Fleetbase Console.
2. Click the **New Part** button.
3. Provide the details:
   - **Name**: A descriptive name for the part.
   - **Part Number / SKU**: The manufacturer's identifier, useful for reordering.
   - **Description**: Any relevant details or compatibility notes.
   - **Unit Cost**: The price you paid for the part. This is used to calculate the total cost of a [Work Order](/docs/fleetops/maintenance/work-orders).
   - **Quantity on Hand**: The current number of these parts you have in stock.
4. Click **Save**.

## Using Parts in Work Orders

The primary way parts are consumed is through Work Orders.

When a mechanic is working on a vehicle and needs to replace a component, they open the active Work Order and add a "Part" line item.

1. In the Work Order, click **Add Part**.
2. Search the catalog for the required part.
3. Enter the quantity used (e.g., 4 spark plugs).
4. Click **Save**.

FleetOps automatically performs two actions:
1. It deducts the quantity used from the part's **Quantity on Hand** in your inventory.
2. It multiplies the quantity by the part's **Unit Cost** and adds that amount to the total cost of the Work Order.

This ensures your inventory levels are always accurate and the true cost of the repair is captured.

## Restocking Parts

When your inventory runs low, you will need to order more parts from a supplier (a [Vendor](/docs/fleetops/management/vendors)).

When the new shipment arrives, you must update the inventory in FleetOps:

1. Open the Part record in the Fleetbase Console.
2. Update the **Quantity on Hand** to reflect the new total.
3. If the price of the part has changed, update the **Unit Cost**. *Note: This will only affect future Work Orders; it will not retroactively change the cost of completed repairs.*
4. Click **Save**.

By keeping your Parts catalog up-to-date, you ensure your maintenance operations run smoothly and your cost tracking remains accurate.
