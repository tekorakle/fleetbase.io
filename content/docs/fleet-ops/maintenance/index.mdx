# Maintenance

Keeping your vehicles on the road is critical to your bottom line. The **Maintenance** section of FleetOps provides a comprehensive Computerized Maintenance Management System (CMMS) built directly into your dispatch operations.

By managing maintenance within FleetOps, you ensure that dispatchers always know which vehicles are available and which are in the shop, preventing the assignment of orders to out-of-service assets.

## Core Maintenance Features

### [Maintenance Schedules](/docs/fleetops/maintenance/maintenance-schedules)
Preventative maintenance is the key to avoiding costly breakdowns. Set up recurring schedules based on time (e.g., every 6 months) or usage (e.g., every 10,000 miles). FleetOps automatically tracks vehicle odometers and alerts you when service is due.

### [Work Orders](/docs/fleetops/maintenance/work-orders)
When a vehicle needs service—whether it's a scheduled oil change or an unexpected repair—you create a Work Order. This tracks the issue, the assigned mechanic, the labor hours, and the total cost of the repair.

### [Parts](/docs/fleetops/maintenance/parts)
Manage your inventory of spare parts (e.g., tires, filters, brake pads). When a mechanic completes a Work Order, they can log which parts were used, automatically deducting them from your inventory and adding their cost to the repair total.

### [Equipment](/docs/fleetops/maintenance/equipment)
Track the specialized tools and equipment used by your mechanics in the shop (e.g., diagnostic scanners, lifts).

## The Maintenance Lifecycle

A typical maintenance workflow in FleetOps looks like this:

1. **Issue Identification**: A driver reports a problem via the Navigator app (creating an [Issue](/docs/fleetops/management/issues)), or a [Maintenance Schedule](/docs/fleetops/maintenance/maintenance-schedules) triggers an alert that service is due.
2. **Work Order Creation**: A fleet manager creates a [Work Order](/docs/fleetops/maintenance/work-orders) and assigns it to a mechanic or an external vendor. The vehicle's status is automatically updated to `maintenance`, preventing dispatchers from assigning it to new orders.
3. **Execution**: The mechanic performs the work, logging their labor hours and any [Parts](/docs/fleetops/maintenance/parts) used.
4. **Completion**: The Work Order is marked as completed. The total cost is calculated, and the vehicle's status is returned to `active`, making it available for dispatch again.

## Integration with Operations

Because Maintenance is built into FleetOps, it shares data seamlessly with your daily operations:

- **Odometer Tracking**: As drivers complete orders, their reported mileage automatically updates the vehicle's odometer, which in turn drives the Maintenance Schedules.
- **Availability**: Dispatchers and the [Scheduler](/docs/fleetops/operations/scheduler) are instantly aware when a vehicle is taken out of service for repairs.
- **Cost Analysis**: The cost of parts and labor from Work Orders is aggregated in the [Analytics](/docs/fleetops/analytics) dashboard, allowing you to calculate the true Total Cost of Ownership (TCO) for every vehicle in your fleet.
