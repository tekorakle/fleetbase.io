# Service Quotes

Before a customer commits to an order, they often want to know how much it will cost. In FleetOps, a **Service Quote** is an estimated price for a delivery, calculated dynamically based on your configured [Service Rates](/docs/fleetops/operations/service-rates).

Service Quotes are essential for providing upfront pricing transparency, especially in on-demand courier or freight operations.

## How Service Quotes Work

A Service Quote is generated by passing the parameters of a potential order (pickup location, dropoff location, and optionally the payload details) through the FleetOps pricing engine.

The engine evaluates these parameters against all active Service Rates. If multiple rates apply (e.g., a "Standard Delivery" rate and an "Express Delivery" rate), the engine can return multiple quotes, allowing the customer to choose the service level they prefer.

## Generating a Quote

Service Quotes are most commonly generated programmatically via the [Core API](/docs/api-reference/core/service-quotes), often triggered by a customer interacting with a frontend application (like the [Storefront](/docs/storefront) app or your own custom portal).

However, dispatchers can also generate quotes manually in the Fleetbase Console:

1. Navigate to **FleetOps** > **Service Quotes** in the Fleetbase Console.
2. Click the **New Quote** button.
3. Enter the **Pickup** and **Dropoff** locations.
4. (Optional) Add **Payload** details (weight, volume) if your Service Rates depend on them.
5. Click **Calculate**.

FleetOps will display the estimated price based on the applicable Service Rates.

## Converting a Quote to an Order

Once a Service Quote is generated, it can be converted directly into an [Order](/docs/fleetops/operations/orders).

When a quote is converted:
1. A new Order is created using the pickup, dropoff, and payload details from the quote.
2. The calculated price from the quote is locked in and attached to the Order.
3. The original Service Quote record is marked as `accepted`.

This ensures that the price the customer was quoted is exactly the price they are charged, even if your underlying Service Rates change before the delivery is completed.

## Quote Expiration

Because logistics costs can fluctuate (e.g., due to surge pricing during peak hours), Service Quotes are typically not valid indefinitely.

When configuring your Service Rates, you can define an expiration period for the quotes they generate (e.g., "Quotes are valid for 15 minutes"). If a customer attempts to convert an expired quote into an order, the system will prompt them to generate a new quote based on the current rates.

## Integration with Storefront

If you are using the **Storefront** extension, the Service Quote system is deeply integrated into the checkout process.

When a customer adds items to their cart and proceeds to checkout, Storefront automatically requests a Service Quote from FleetOps based on the customer's delivery address and the total weight/volume of the items in the cart. The resulting quote is then displayed to the customer as the "Delivery Fee" before they complete their purchase.
