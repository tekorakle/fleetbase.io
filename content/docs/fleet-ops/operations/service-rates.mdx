# Service Rates

Pricing logistics services is rarely as simple as a flat fee. The cost of a delivery often depends on the distance traveled, the weight of the goods, the time of day, and the specific regions involved.

**Service Rates** in FleetOps allow you to build complex, dynamic pricing models that automatically calculate the cost of an order based on its specific parameters.

## How Service Rates Work

A Service Rate is essentially a formula. When an order is created (or when a [Service Quote](/docs/fleetops/operations/service-quotes) is requested), FleetOps evaluates the order against your defined Service Rates to determine the final price.

A Service Rate consists of:
- **Base Fee**: A fixed starting price for the service.
- **Rate Calculation Method**: How the variable portion of the price is calculated (e.g., per kilometer, per kilogram).
- **Conditions**: Rules that determine when this specific rate applies (e.g., only for orders within a specific Zone, or only for a specific Customer).

## Creating a Service Rate

To define a new pricing model:

1. Navigate to **FleetOps** > **Service Rates** in the Fleetbase Console.
2. Click the **New Service Rate** button.
3. Provide a **Name** for the rate (e.g., "Standard Local Delivery", "Heavy Freight - Zone A").
4. Set the **Base Fee**.
5. Define the **Calculation Method** and its associated fees.
6. (Optional) Add **Conditions** to restrict when this rate applies.
7. Click **Save**.

## Calculation Methods

FleetOps supports several methods for calculating the variable cost of a delivery:

### Distance-Based Pricing
The most common method. You define a price per unit of distance (e.g., $1.50 per kilometer). FleetOps uses its routing engine to calculate the driving distance between the pickup and dropoff locations, and multiplies it by your rate.

### Weight/Volume-Based Pricing
Useful for freight and courier services. You define a price per unit of weight (e.g., $0.50 per kilogram) or volume. FleetOps multiplies this rate by the total weight/volume of the items defined in the order's Payload.

### Zone-Based Pricing
Often used for local deliveries. You define specific geographic [Zones](/docs/fleetops/operations/zones) (e.g., "Downtown", "Suburbs"). You can then set flat rates for deliveries within a single zone, or specific rates for crossing between zones (e.g., Pickup in Zone A, Dropoff in Zone B = $25).

### Time-Based Pricing
You can define a price per hour or minute of estimated driving time.

## Conditions and Overrides

You can create multiple Service Rates and use Conditions to ensure the correct one is applied to each order.

Common conditions include:
- **Customer Specific**: Create a discounted Service Rate that only applies when a specific [Contact](/docs/fleetops/management/contacts) is the customer.
- **Vehicle Type**: Apply a higher rate if the order requires a specialized vehicle (e.g., a refrigerated truck).
- **Time of Day/Week**: Apply a surcharge for deliveries requested outside of normal business hours or on weekends.

If an order matches the conditions for multiple Service Rates, FleetOps will typically apply the most specific rate, or you can manually select the desired rate during order creation.

## Service Quotes

Service Rates are the engine behind **Service Quotes**.

Before a customer commits to an order, they often want to know how much it will cost. By providing the pickup and dropoff locations (and optionally the payload details), FleetOps can instantly generate a Service Quote by running those parameters through your active Service Rates.

This is particularly powerful when integrated with the [Storefront](/docs/storefront) extension or your own custom customer portal via the [Core API](/docs/api-reference/core/service-quotes).
